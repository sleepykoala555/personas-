0. Introduction:

In this Operation Fried Waffles, you will uncover a “secret” of Brown’s Science Libary (known as “Scili”), and follow the clues to unveil the identity of the legendary Dr. Fitz. To accomplish this mission, you need to explore the different floors of Scili, so the elevators will be your best friend.

Thus, you’ll first need to learn about the control panels of the elevators and their limitations, by observing and interviewing three people – Lisa, Sunny, and Jerry, about their experiences of interacting with the interfaces. After that, you’ll befriend two personas that typify common user behaviors and frustrations. And finally, you’ll join Ben’s adventure and follow his journey to unlock various locations in Scili and find the legendary Dr. Fitz.

The purpose of this exercise is to allow you to better understand how users interact with a common interface, as banal as the button panel of an elevator, and think about how details matter.

Let’s begin!


1. Selected Interface:

Button panels of the elevators in the Science Library ("Scili") at Brown University.

2. Description and Annotated Sketch:

The button panels of the elevators in the Science Library at Brown consist of:

  • Interface 1: Up/down button panel that allows us to tell the elevators that we want to go up or down, and a light bar display that allows us to get noticed which of the three elevators is approaching soon.
  • Interface 2: Floor number button panel and the floor number display, that allows us to instruct the elevators to take us to a certain floor/level, and see our current position.

3. Observations and Interview Summaries:


3a) Summary Table


  Observations Interviews responses
Lisa

1) Lisa tries to go to the third floor from the first floor to see Doctor Geraldine Fitzgerald, the salamander in the Science Centre (floor 3) on a Friday night.

2) However when in the elevator, she presses floor 3 but the button doesn't light up.

3) Lisa tried using the stairs to go to floor 3 but still could not open the door.

4) Lisa goes back to the ground level instead and asks the guard instead. She is told the Science Center is closed over the weekend.

5) “That’s why I don’t like coming here,” Lisa comments.

1) Lisa is a fourth-year grad student from China. Lisa comes to the Science Library only 2-3 times per year, the least frequent among the 3 interviewees.

2) As the only interviewee who went to a restricted level(floor 9) for an invited event before, she had to swipe her student card before pressing the floor number button, a confusing experience for her.

3) Lisa is confused about the meanings of L, A, and M. She is used to seeing G for the ground floor, LL for the basement, and 2 for the mezzanine.

4) Lisa did not know which floors are accessible and what functions they serve given the limited info on the interfaces, and thus had very limited usage and knowledge of the lding. Several times, she had to turn to the guard/receptionist for help to know which floor she should go to.

5) Lisa suggests that labels can be put with numbers, and have some signs to indicate accessibility.

Sunny

1) Sunny is waiting for the elevator on the 13th floor after finishing studying and wants to go to the ground floor to grap a quick lunch.

2) After about 30s waiting, Sunny starts to feel impatient so she decides to quickly fill her water bottles and come back.

3) When she’s back, the elevator has left so she has to press the button and wait again.

4) “Damn, again!?” Sunny signed.

1) Sunny is a senior from Singapore. She comes to the Scili frequently for a variety of reasons, and she has explored many different floors.

2) Sunny was first confused about the meanings of L, A, and M. She would use B for the basement, G or 1 for the ground floor, and 2 for the mezzanine.

3) Sunny did not know which floors are accessible and usable in the first place so she tried pressing all of the buttons to see which ones would work, which was frustrating. She attributed the underuse of Scili by many students to limited information on the interfaces.

4) Sunny enjoyed going to the 12th/13th floor to study in the cubicles, a place unknown to many students. However, waiting for elevators on these two floors is frustrating and can a long time.

5) Sunny suggests putting up a QR code for detailed info outside the elevator/on the elevator’s door for accessibility, opening times, and functions of each floor. Also, Sunny wants to see which floors the elevators are to better estimate the waiting time.

Jerry

1) After going to the writing center for the first time, Jerry wants to find a place for self-studying in Scili.

2) Entering the lyft, Jerry is unsure about which floor to go. He’s not sure about what are floor A and floor M.

3) He takes out his phone and starts googling. In the end, he decides that he wants to go to floor 4, the quiet studying floor.

4) However, the rooms and tables are filled so he has to leave and presses both floors M and A to see.

1) Jerry is a freshman student from Michigan. He has only visited the Scili one or two times during the orientation.

2) He vividly remembered the first time he went to Scili, he had no idea of what the M meant and felt frustrated by the fact he could not find floor 2.

3) Jerry thought only levels A, L, and M are accessible to students so that’s why letters are used, while all other floors have restricted access.

4) Jerry agrees waiting for Scili’s elevators is frustrating because cannot see their positions.

5) Jerry suggests that relevant information could have been provided during the orientation because the elevators in Scili are old so their interfaces are old and confusing to use.
Patterns

Interface 1:

  • When the waiting time is long, people would display some agitation/impatience when waiting for the elevators.

Interface 2:

  • New students tend to be more unfamiliar with using the elevators while old students may only go to several floors without exploring the rest.

Interface 1:

  • Common frustrations are from the lack of a digital display. Corresponds with observations.
  • The light bar and “bee” sound are useful.

Interface 2:

  • Everyone was first confused by the use of letters A, L, and M, especially some international students who are used to different conventions.
  • Everyone feels discouraged to explore other floors of Scili to some degree because of a lack of information on the interfaces.


3b) Observation and Interview Questions:

3bi) Observations Guidelines:

  1. [Interface 1] Before entering the elevator:

    1. Which floor it is? Which floor is he/she trying to get to?
    2. Does the person hesitate/choose between the elevator and the stairs?
    3. Does the person look at the directory or search on phone/other devicesto look for floor information before entering the elevator?
    4. Does he/she press the button to go up or down?
    5. Does he/she display any impatience/agitation while waiting for theelevator to come?
    6. How does he/she react when hearing the “bee” sound and seeing thelight bar lit up and turn red?

  2. [Interface 2] After entering the elevator:

    1. Does the person hesitate before pressing the floor number buttons? Or is it something very natural and familiar to them?
    2. Which floor does he/she press? Did he/she press A, L, M? Does he/sheexperience any confusion?

  3. [Interface 2] Approaching Task and Navigating Confusion:

    (Note. I gave every person a task, try to find out an alternative floor they have access to for self-studying/room-reservation but did not previously know. Observing and recording their response)

    1. Does the person already know which alternative floor he/she can go to? What does he/she do to find out?
    2. How does he/she interact with the button panel when navigating through the confusion?

  4. [Post-Interaction] Comparisons with other Users and Key Insights:

    1. What are some of the general patterns observed from this user?
    2. In what ways are the users’ behaviors and interactions different from others?

3bii) Interviews Guidelines:

  1. Basic information – User Profile:

    1. Where are you from? Where did you stay on a long-term basis beforebecoming Brown?
    2. What year are you? How long have you been staying at Brown University?
    3. What have been the reasons that you come to the Science Library(self-studying, meeting with friends, having classes, TA hours,extracurriculars, borrowing/lending books, etc.)? How frequently do you use the Science Library?
    4. Which floors do you have you been to? Which floors do you visit the most frequently?
    5. How often do you use the elevators versus the stairs? How do you choose between?

  2. Waiting for Elevators:

    1. Have you had a situation where you pressed the wrong button(up/down)? What do you do? If not, how would you imagine your reaction?
    2. How do you feel about the waiting time? How do you feel, and what do you do if the elevator still hasn’t come after you’ve been waiting for a while after pressing the button?

  3. Use of Letter Representations on Button Panel:

    1. Did you ever have trouble selecting which button to press(A, L, M)? Did you know what the letters (A, L, M) mean? If so, how did you know(from previous experiences, etc..)? If not, how did you find out later?
    2. What are the conventions for labeling floors A(basement), L(lobby, ground level), and M(floor 2, mezzanine) from your previous experiences/cultures? What are you used to?
    3. What happens when you press the wrong floor number/button? How would you react to it?

  4. Information regarding Floor Accessibility and Functions:

    1. Since you first came to the school and navigated the Science Library with the elevator, did you ever have any difficulty knowing which floor to go to? If yes, how did you figure it out? If not, what was the main struggle/confusion for you?
    2. Do you know what floors are accessible and what floors have restricted access? How did you find out? (if this ever happened)What happened after
    3. you found out a floor/some floors are inaccessible? If you ever needed to go to a restricted floor, how did you gain access? Other than the floors you visit the most/visited before, do you know about the functions and usage of other floors? If yes, How did you go about finding out the information? If not, have you wondered or wanted to explore other floors, and what stopped you?
    4. Fun fact test: Do you know who is Doctor Geraldine Fitzgerald and where can we find her in the Scili? Do you know what will be the days and times you can visit her?

    Dr. Fitz, Geraldine Fitzgerald, PH.D.

  5. Suggestions and Additional Information:

    1. What changes in design could have been implemented to interface 1 to improve the experience of waiting for the elevators?
    2. What could have been done to interface 2 to make the experience of using the button panel better and smoother for you(accurately pressing the right button without confusion)?
    3. What changes could have been made to the button panel to help you better navigate and utilize the Science Library(knowing the accessibility and function of each floor)?
    4. Other than the aspects mentioned above, are there other things about the interfaces that confuse you and/or delight you?
    5. Any additional features or functions you want to see on the interfaces? (Can be very creative here)
    6. Other feedback, stories, or relevant information?
